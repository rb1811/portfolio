Wealth Architecture

RecentlI've recently started prioritizing genuine wealth creation, inspired by foundational financial texts. Reading books like The Richest Man in Babylon, The Psychology of Money, and Rich Dad Poor Dad has been eye-opening. The core lesson is clear: relying solely on the intense '996' work culture primarily serves to maximize the wealth of C-suite executives, often leaving me, the dedicated employee, perpetually chasing my own financial and life stability. My goal now is to establish a working relationship—whether as an employee or a freelancer—that is mutually beneficial and values my contribution as a true partner, not just a resource.

The Value of Decency

I value a high-paying job, but it is utterly secondary to foundational human dignity: a stress-free commute, breathable air, guaranteed drinking water in summer, zero water-logging in the rains, and freedom from the 'Rental Mafia.' No exorbitant salary in a poorly managed city can compel me to compromise on this baseline decency. When you have the opportunity and means to build multiple income streams, you deserve to live as a human, not as a slave to location or circumstance.
